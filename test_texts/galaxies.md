# Spiral galaxies

Spiral galaxies are rotating systems made of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter.
Their bright arms trace regions where new stars are actively forming.

Astronomers study galaxy rotation curves because the visible matter alone does not explain the observed speeds.
Those measurements became one of the key clues that dark matter exists.

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy.
Its central bar helps funnel gas inward and influences the shape of the inner arms.
